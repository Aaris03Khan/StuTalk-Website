“The City That Never Sleeps”- How True Is It?

  • Akkaldevi Hemanth
  • Class of 2021

People often make plans for a long ride, be it north or south. But the one thing you need to consider is what that place has to offer you which makes your trip worthy. Here is my take on the front row-experience I had in exploring the City That Never Sleeps.

What does it take have a good trip? For starters, a good plan, a sound wallet, and a group of people who will make the trip fun. Once you’ve got this starter pack on, there’s nothing in this world that can stop you except for the traffic cops or a bad weather (if you know, you know).

Mine was pretty simple. I packed some bags, called up a few friends, fueled up and hit the road. But they say life is all about how unexpected it can get. And so did mine! Halfway through the trip, I had my cousins join me.

Right when it started to feel like a smooth ride my motorcycle broke down out of the blue.

There was no human trace at all, let alone a mechanic. That had me bring out the mechanical engineer within me. Once that was fixed, we hit the roads again and reached our destination. Within hours we reached Mumbai. And yes, everything we’ve heard about that city was true! The place, the people, it never sleeps. Mumbai is always a happening city irrespective of the time and place. It is filled with nice and fun people, too.

We got to stay there for around 5 days which was more than what we had planned on initially. But, I gotta say, each and every day was worth it. The best part of all this is that we got to meet a wonderful set of people who not only treated us well, but also took great care of us. I'd say, it was one ride of my life that I will always cherish.

On the whole, it was one hell of a trip! And if visiting Mumbai is in your bucketlist, I promise you, you wouldn't be disappointed; rather you'd be surprised at the accuracy of my description here.
